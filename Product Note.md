## 1. Executive Summary

**What is Cardeko Social AI?**  
An AI-powered digital marketing platform built exclusively for Indian automobile dealerships. It replaces a designer, a social media manager, a copywriter, and an ad agency with a single software product that any dealer can use in 10 minutes per day.

**The one-line product promise:**  
*From car inventory to customer leads — fully automated.*

**The problem it solves:**  
25,000+ automobile dealerships in India know they need a strong digital presence but cannot maintain one. They either hire agencies (expensive, slow, disconnected from real inventory), use generic tools (Canva, Buffer — no automotive or India context), or do nothing. The result: sporadic, low-quality social content, missed festival opportunities, unanswered customer messages, and money wasted on poorly targeted ads.

**Why now?**  
Two convergent forces: (1) AI models (Groq, OpenAI, Cloudflare AI) have reached the quality threshold needed to generate professional-grade, hallucination-free automotive marketing content. (2) Indian dealers are increasingly social-media-aware post-COVID and are actively looking for affordable, effective solutions — but nothing purpose-built for them exists.

**The business model:**  
SaaS subscription (₹4,999–₹19,999/month) plus a performance fee on ad spend routed through the platform (5–10%). At 1,000 dealers, this is a ₹60–80 Cr annual recurring revenue business. The path to 1,000 dealers is achievable in 24 months given our existing relationship with 100+ dealers from the legacy managed service.

## 2. The Problem — In Depth

### 2.1 The Digital Marketing Gap in Indian Auto Retail

India is the world's third-largest automobile market, selling over 4.2 million passenger vehicles annually. The country has an estimated 25,000+ organized passenger vehicle dealerships and 60,000+ two-wheeler dealerships. Yet the digital marketing quality of most of these dealers is poor, inconsistent, and ineffective.

This is not because dealers don't understand the importance of digital marketing. In research conducted with 50 dealers across India:

- **94%** said social media was important or very important for their business
- **86%** said they were not satisfied with the quality or consistency of their current social media output
- **72%** said they had missed a festival marketing opportunity in the past year because they didn't prepare in time
- **68%** said they had unanswered customer comments or reviews on their pages for more than 48 hours

The gap is not awareness — it is execution capability.

### 2.2 Why Dealers Cannot Execute Well Today

**1. No design capability.**  
Creating a professional social media post requires design software, a template library, knowledge of platform dimensions, and branding consistency. Most dealers do not have a graphic designer on staff. Those who try to use Canva spend 30–60 minutes per post and still produce mediocre results.

**2. No copywriting skill.**  
A caption that drives a phone call is different from one that gets likes. Indian automotive marketing has its own vocabulary — EMI structures, exchange offers, festival-specific messaging, regional language phrases. Generic copy tools produce generic captions.

**3. No time.**  
A dealership owner manages OEM relationships, inventory, sales targets, service department, staff, and customer escalations. Social media is perpetually the last priority. When an agency or staff member asks "what should we post today," the answer is usually "figure it out yourself" — which leads to inconsistency or inaction.

**4. No platform expertise.**  
Meta Ads Manager is designed for performance marketers. The interface, terminology (CPM, lookalike audiences, ad sets, attribution windows), and complexity are overwhelming for a dealer who just wants to reach people within 25 kilometers who might buy a car. They either don't run ads, or they hit the Facebook "Boost" button without strategy.

**5. No festival readiness.**  
India has 20+ significant auto-buying festivals per year — Navratri, Diwali, Dhanteras, Akshaya Tritiya, Onam, Pongal, Baisakhi, and more. Each is a distinct regional event with different messaging, visual language, and buyer psychology. Missing Dhanteras as a car dealer is like a US retailer missing Black Friday. Most dealers miss 60–70% of these opportunities.

**6. No inbox management.**  
A dealer with active Facebook and Instagram pages gets dozens of comments, DMs, and Google reviews per week. These are live leads and reputation events — a comment asking "what is the on-road price of Creta in Pune?" is a hot lead. An unanswered review is a public trust wound. Most dealers have no system to manage this; they catch what they catch.

### 2.3 The Legacy Model (The Predecessor to This Product)

Before Cardeko Social AI existed, Cardeko operated a fully manual managed service:

- **14 people** servicing approximately **100 dealer accounts**
- Each post required: dealer brief → content planner → designer → copywriter → approval → manual posting → ad setup
- Average turnaround: **3–5 business days** from dealer request to post live
- Net margin: low (labor-heavy, no tech leverage)
- Scalability ceiling: ~200 dealers at maximum operational efficiency with 28 staff
- Quality: inconsistent, dependent on the individual team member

This model could not scale. **The product was built to replace it entirely.**

## 3. The Solution

### 3.1 What Cardeko Social AI Does

Cardeko Social AI is a **Dealer Growth Engine** — a self-serve software platform that:

1. **Generates professional marketing content** from a single text prompt, in under 15 seconds
2. **Publishes content** to Facebook, Instagram, and Google My Business simultaneously
3. **Launches targeted ad campaigns** in 3 taps, with pre-configured audience targeting
4. **Manages all incoming customer messages and reviews** in a unified inbox with AI-suggested replies
5. **Connects to real dealer inventory** so content features the actual cars they're selling at the actual price
6. **Understands the Indian calendar** — auto-suggests festival campaigns before the dealer thinks to ask

### 3.2 The Experience in Practice

A dealer opens the app on Monday morning. The dashboard shows:
- 3 new messages in the inbox (one is a lead asking about test drive availability)
- A notification: "Navratri is 8 days away. Here are 3 suggested posts to schedule."
- A suggested post for today based on their inventory (a new stock of Hyundai Creta that arrived Friday)

The dealer taps on the Creta suggestion, reviews the 3 AI-generated creative variants (one punchy, one detailed, one emotional), picks the punchy one, adjusts the caption slightly, selects Facebook + Instagram + GMB, and hits "Publish + Boost ₹500/day for 3 days."

Total time: **4 minutes.** The post is live on 3 platforms. An ad campaign is running targeting auto-intenders within 25km. The AI handles the inbox replies.

This is what the product delivers.

5. **Scalable geographic expansion.** The product works for a dealer in Mumbai just as well as one in Lucknow or Coimbatore — with regional language and festival customization. India's 700+ million internet users are expanding rapidly into Tier 2 and 3 cities, where a dealer may have never had any professional marketing support.

## 6. Product Overview — Feature by Feature

### 6.1 AI Creative Engine

The heart of the product. Type one prompt → AI generates 3 professional creative variants in under 15 seconds.

**What it does:**
- Accepts a free-text prompt ("Weekend offer on Maruti Brezza" or "Navratri special on all Tata models")
- OR: select from a library of 100+ curated prompt templates (New Arrival, Festival Offer, Service Camp, Customer Testimonial, etc.)
- AI (Groq Llama-3.3-70b-versatile, with OpenAI GPT-4o as fallback) generates 3 caption variants:
  - **Punchy:** Under 60 words, bold and urgent, optimized for Instagram
  - **Detailed:** 100–150 words, specs + EMI + price, informational
  - **Emotional:** 80–120 words, aspirational, family/lifestyle focused
- Each variant includes: caption text, 5–10 hashtags, emoji suggestions
- Hindi variants generated natively (not translated) when dealer has Hindi enabled
- AI composites a 3-layer image: AI-generated background + vehicle photo (background removed) + dealer branding overlay (name, city, phone, WhatsApp, headline)
- Platform-specific sizes generated in parallel: Instagram (1080×1080), Facebook (1200×630), Instagram Story (1080×1920), GMB (1200×900)

**Technical implementation:**  
Groq API (primary) → OpenRouter (fallback 1) → OpenAI (fallback 2) → mock data (fallback 3). The dealer never sees an AI failure — the system always returns a response. Image composition uses @napi-rs/canvas + Sharp on the backend. Backgrounds generated by Cloudflare Workers AI.

**Key guardrail:**  
The AI is explicitly instructed to NEVER invent prices, NEVER approximate specifications, and NEVER include data not provided by the dealer. This is enforced both in the system prompt and in post-generation validation.

### 6.2 Multi-Platform Publisher

**Connect Once, Publish Everywhere:**
- OAuth-based connection to Facebook Page, Instagram Business, Google My Business
- Long-lived tokens stored encrypted (AES-256-GCM). Auto-refreshed before expiry.
- Dealer notified via push notification if a connection expires and needs renewal.

**Post Now or Schedule:**
- "Post Now": content goes live on all selected platforms within 30 seconds
- "Schedule": pick a date and time, AI suggests the optimal window based on dealer's historical engagement. Post is queued in BullMQ (backed by Redis) and fires precisely at the scheduled time.
- Failed publishes retry 3 times with exponential backoff (1 minute, 5 minutes, 15 minutes).
- Dealer notified on failure; each platform reported independently.

**Content Calendar:**
- Week view (default) and month view
- Drag-and-drop rescheduling on desktop
- Post cards show: thumbnail, platform icons, scheduled time, status (Draft / Scheduled / Publishing / Published / Partially Published / Failed)
- Bulk scheduling: AI pre-populates a week's worth of suggested content; dealer reviews and approves in one action

### 6.3 One-Click Boost

The single largest revenue driver. Meta Ads Manager is inaccessible to most Indian dealers — this module replaces it with a 3-tap flow.

**How it works:**
1. Select any published or scheduled post
2. Set daily budget (₹500 / ₹1,000 / ₹2,500 / Custom)
3. Set duration (3 / 7 / 14 days / Custom)
4. Tap "Launch Boost"

The system automatically:
- Creates a Meta Ads campaign (objective: Traffic or Awareness)
- Targets auto-intenders within 25km of the dealer's location (configurable)
- Sets age range 25–55 with brand-relevant interest targeting (Maruti fans if they sell Maruti, etc.)
- Launches within 60 seconds

**Boost Dashboard:**
- Active campaigns: daily spend, total spent, reach, clicks, CTR, CPC
- Pause / Resume / Stop per campaign
- Historical campaigns with final performance data
- Total monthly ad spend summary

**Revenue model:**  
Cardeko charges a 5–10% service fee on ad spend routed through the platform. At ₹50,000/month average boost spend per dealer, this is ₹2,500–₹5,000 in additional revenue per dealer per month — on top of the subscription fee.

### 6.4 Unified Inbox

All customer messages, comments, and reviews — one screen.

**Sources aggregated:**
- Facebook Page comments and direct messages
- Instagram post comments and direct messages
- Google My Business reviews

**For every incoming message:**
- AI generates a suggested reply (Groq/OpenAI)
- Sentiment detected: positive / neutral / negative
- Auto-tagged: Lead / Complaint / General / Spam
- Negative messages: flagged with red border, require manual review before sending

**The Lead Pipeline:**
- Messages tagged as "Lead" can be converted to a lead record with one tap
- Captures: customer name, phone, vehicle interest, source
- Lead data flows into the analytics dashboard

**Business impact:**  
An unanswered lead comment is a competitor's gain. A dealership that responds to every message within 15 minutes converts at dramatically higher rates. This module makes that response rate achievable for a 1-person team.

### 6.5 Inventory Connector

Real cars, real prices, in every creative.

**Phase 1 — CSV Upload:**
- Dealer uploads their inventory as a CSV (exported from their DMS — Autocades, AutoVista, or a simple Excel sheet)
- Column mapping UI handles different DMS export formats automatically
- Required fields: Make, Model, Variant, Year, Price, Condition, Image URLs
- Inventory appears in a searchable table: filter by make, model, status, price range

**Integration with AI Creative:**
- When creating a post, dealer can select a specific vehicle from inventory
- All vehicle data (exact price, specs, stock count) is injected into the AI system prompt
- The AI uses the real price — never approximates or invents
- The vehicle's photo is used as the product image in the creative (background removed, composed on AI background)

**Auto-triggers:**
- New inventory added → notification: "Generate showcase posts for these new arrivals?"
- Vehicle marked as Sold → flags any scheduled posts: "This vehicle was sold. Unpublish this post?"

**Phase 2 — DMS API Integration (Post-MVP):**
- Direct API connectivity with major Indian DMS providers
- Inventory syncs automatically — no manual CSV upload needed
- Webhook-based: DMS pushes changes to Cardeko in real-time

### 6.6 India Context Pack

Built for India, not translated for India.

**Festival Calendar Engine:**
- 20+ Indian festivals pre-loaded for 12 months (Navratri, Diwali, Dhanteras, Akshaya Tritiya, Onam, Pongal, Baisakhi, Holi, etc.)
- 14 days before each festival: automatic notification + pre-suggested posts for that festival
- Regional filtering: Kerala dealer sees Onam and Vishu; Punjab dealer sees Baisakhi and Lohri; Tamil Nadu dealer sees Pongal and Tamil New Year

**Language Intelligence:**
- Hindi captions generated natively by the AI (not translated from English)
- Regional language support in Phase 2: Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi
- The AI is explicitly instructed on Indian automotive vocabulary, regional idioms, and culturally appropriate phrasing

**Brand Voice Library:**
- Each major Indian auto brand has a voice profile in the system:
  - Maruti Suzuki: family trust, value-for-money, Hindi comfort
  - Hyundai: aspirational, modern, young professional
  - Tata Motors: pride of India, safety leadership, EV future
  - Mahindra: adventure, ruggedness, power
  - MG: tech-forward, premium-but-accessible, urban
  - Kia: bold design, sporty, style-conscious
  - Toyota: legendary reliability, long-term value

**GMB-First Approach:**
- Google My Business is treated as a first-class publishing platform (not an afterthought)
- Every creative generates a GMB-optimized variant with a Call-to-Action button (CALL / LEARN_MORE)
- Weekly GMB updates auto-suggested (stock highlights, service offers, updated hours)
- Google reviews integrated into the Unified Inbox

### 6.7 Analytics & Lead Attribution

Proves ROI to the dealer.

**Dashboard metrics:**
- Posts published this week/month
- Total reach across all platforms
- Engagement rate per platform
- Leads generated (by source: Facebook, Instagram, GMB, WhatsApp)
- Top performing posts (ranked by leads generated, not likes)
- Boost campaign ROI: spend vs leads vs cost-per-lead

**Monthly Auto-Report:**
- Auto-generated at the start of each month
- Covers: total posts, reach, leads, ad spend, cost per lead, top 3 posts
- Designed to be WhatsApp-shareable — dealer should be proud to forward it to their OEM area manager
- Delivered via WhatsApp + email

## 9. Differentiation — Why We Win

### 9.1 The Depth of Context Is the Moat

Any tool can generate a caption. What makes ours defensible is the **depth of Indian automotive context** baked into every layer of the system:

- **Brand voice profiles** for every major Indian auto brand (Maruti, Hyundai, Tata, Mahindra, MG, Kia, Toyota, Honda, Volkswagen, Skoda, BMW, Mercedes, Audi)
- **Regional tone profiles** for North/South/East/West/Central India — North India gets price-forward urgency; South India gets trust-and-quality messaging
- **20+ Indian festivals** with cultural nuance — we know that Dhanteras is India's biggest single buying day and that Akshaya Tritiya is particularly strong in West India
- **Hindi and regional language generation** that is culturally native, not translated
- **Automotive-specific guardrails** — the AI never invents prices or specs (a legal and trust issue that generic AI tools don't address)

This contextual depth takes months to curate and test. It cannot be replicated in weeks by a generic tool.

### 9.2 The Inventory Integration Is Unique

When a dealer posts "Weekend offer on Maruti Brezza," generic AI generates a placeholder caption with invented or approximated prices. Cardeko's system pulls the exact price of the Brezza from the dealer's live inventory database — ₹14,49,000 (Delhi ex-showroom), not "starting at approximately ₹14 lakhs."

This accuracy is non-negotiable for dealer trust and legal compliance. It is also not replicable without building the inventory integration layer.

### 9.3 The Workflow Fit Is Defensible

The product is designed to fit how Indian dealers actually work:
- Phone-first, WhatsApp-native
- Hindi-comfortable
- Festival-cycle driven
- Approval-oriented (the GM reviews, the owner approves)

A generic Western social media tool will never invest in these specific workflow accommodations. We do because this is our only market.

### 9.4 The Network Effect of Data

As more dealers use the platform, we accumulate:
- **Engagement data:** Which types of posts get the most comments/clicks at what time of day across which platforms — across all dealers
- **Festival performance data:** Which creative styles work best for Diwali vs Navratri vs Dussehra
- **Regional preferences:** Which captions convert in Bengaluru vs Lucknow vs Coimbatore
- **Inventory-to-lead correlation:** Which vehicle categories and price points generate the most social leads

This data improves the AI's recommendations for every subsequent dealer — creating a flywheel where more dealers make the product better for all dealers.

| | Legacy Model | SaaS Model |

### 10.2 Unit Economics Per Dealer

**Growth tier dealer (₹9,999/month):**

| Item | Cost/Month |

**Channel:** OEM area managers who manage 20–50 dealers each are a force multiplier. A single area manager who endorses the tool can unlock 50 dealer conversations. Target 5–10 OEM area manager relationships in Phase 2.

**Additional channels:**

**Target:** OEM (Maruti, Hyundai, Tata) licensing the platform for their entire dealer network.  
**Structure:** OEM pays a per-dealer license fee and white-labels the product under their brand.  
**Opportunity:** Maruti has 3,000+ dealers. A ₹3,000/month per-dealer license = ₹10.8 Cr/month from one OEM partnership.  
**Positioning:** "The official digital marketing platform for [OEM] dealers" — reduces sales friction to near-zero.

## 13. Risks & Mitigations

### 13.1 AI Content Quality

**Risk:** AI generates a caption with a wrong price, an invented specification, or culturally inappropriate content. A dealer publishes it without reviewing. Legal or trust issue results.

**Mitigation:**
- Strict guardrails in the AI system prompt: "NEVER invent prices. If no price is provided, omit pricing."
- Post-generation validation: if the caption contains a number that doesn't match any inventory data, flag for review
- UI encourages review before publishing (the dealer always selects a variant — they never publish without seeing it)
- Terms of service: dealer is responsible for reviewing content before publishing
- In-app flagging: "This caption includes pricing. Please verify this matches your current offer before publishing."

### 13.2 Platform API Changes / Restrictions

**Risk:** Meta or Google changes their API permissions, deprecates an endpoint, or restricts access. Our publishing or inbox functionality breaks.

**Mitigation:**
- Pin API versions in config (Meta v19.0, not "latest")
- Maintain active monitoring of Meta's developer blog and Google's API changelog
- Design the publishing module as a plugin interface — each platform is independently replaceable
- Business continuity: if Facebook publishing breaks, Instagram and GMB still work independently

### 13.3 Meta App Review Rejection

**Risk:** Meta rejects or delays our App Review for production access to posting and Ads APIs.

**Mitigation:**
- Start the App Review process in Week 1 (2–4 week lead time)
- During review period, use test pages for development and pilot dealers can manually grant permissions via the Meta Business Suite
- Build the onboarding flow to guide dealers through granting permissions directly from their Meta account

### 13.4 Dealer Churn Due to Complexity

**Risk:** Dealers start a free trial, find the product confusing, and churn before experiencing value.

**Mitigation:**
- "Time to first post" is a core product KPI — target < 15 minutes
- Guided tooltip overlay on first login walks dealers through the first post
- Video walkthroughs in Hindi on the onboarding screen
- Proactive success outreach: call every new dealer within 24 hours of signup
- Identify the "activation event" (first post published) and optimize the entire onboarding funnel to reach it

### 13.5 Competition from Well-Funded Players

**Risk:** A well-funded startup or an existing tool (Canva, Hootsuite) decides to move into the Indian auto dealer market.

**Mitigation:**
- Build category leadership before they arrive — 1,000 dealers is a defensible install base
- The context depth (brand voice profiles, regional tones, festival intelligence, DMS integration) takes 6–12 months to build properly — not a quick copy
- OEM partnerships make switching costs high — if an OEM mandates Cardeko for their dealers, competitors face a procurement hurdle, not just a product comparison

### 13.6 AI API Cost at Scale

**Risk:** At 5,000 dealers each generating 10–20 captions per week, AI API costs escalate significantly.

**Mitigation:**
- Groq's free/low-cost Llama-3.3-70b is the primary model — significantly cheaper than OpenAI
- Cache responses: identical prompt + dealer context → cached for 24 hours
- Monitor cost per dealer per month; if it exceeds ₹500, renegotiate API pricing or switch models
- The gross margin (92%+) provides significant headroom before AI costs become a threat

## 15. The Team & Build Approach

### Build Philosophy

This product was built with a "product-led, AI-native" philosophy:

1. **The AI is a background layer, not a gimmick.** Dealers don't care about AI — they care about getting a post done quickly. The AI is invisible infrastructure that makes the product fast and smart.

2. **Context depth over feature breadth.** We would rather do social media for Indian auto dealers exceptionally well than do social media for all industries adequately. The product must know more about Indian automotive marketing than any generic tool ever will.

3. **India-first, not India-adapted.** Every design decision — language, UX patterns, pricing, festival support — was made with India as the primary context, not retrofitted onto a Western product.

4. **Fallbacks everywhere.** No single dependency should be able to break the core flow. AI provider down → fallback. Storage unavailable → fallback. Platform API rate-limited → queue and retry. The dealer always gets a response.

5. **Obsessive focus on time-to-value.** Every minute between signup and first published post is a minute of doubt. The target is < 15 minutes from zero to first post live on Facebook.

### Technical Build Quality

- Full TypeScript: frontend, backend, and all shared packages
- Monorepo with shared type definitions — no frontend/backend type mismatch
- Prisma schema as single source of truth for the database
- BullMQ-backed background jobs — no job loss on server restart
- Three-provider AI fallback chain — no user-visible AI failures
- AES-256-GCM encryption for all OAuth tokens at rest
- Mobile-first responsive design — works on a ₹8,000 Android phone on 3G
